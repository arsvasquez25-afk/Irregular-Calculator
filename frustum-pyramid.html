Frustum of a Pyramid

A pyramid with its apex sliced off by a horizontal cut, leaving two polygonal bases — one larger at the bottom, one smaller on top.

🏛️ What is a Frustum of a Pyramid?

Picture the Great Pyramid of Giza. Now imagine slicing off its pointed peak with a perfectly horizontal cut. The remaining structure — with a large rectangular base at the bottom and a smaller rectangle on top — is called a frustum of a pyramid.


Unlike the cone frustum (which uses radii), the pyramid frustum uses base areas directly — because the bases can be any polygon: square, rectangle, hexagon, or even irregular.

📐 The Formula

V = (h/3) × (A1 + A2 + √(A1 × A2))
VariableMeaning
VVolume — the total space inside the frustum
hHeight — vertical distance between the two bases
A1Area of the larger base (bottom)
A2Area of the smaller base (top)
√(A1×A2)Geometric mean — accounts for the smooth taper between the two bases

🧠 How the Formula Works (Step by Step)

1

Calculate A1 (area of the big base) and A2 (area of the small base) separately. For a rectangle, that's simply length × width.

2

Compute √(A1 × A2) — the geometric mean of the two areas. This represents the average cross-sectional area at the midpoint of the taper.

3

Add all three: A1 + A2 + √(A1 × A2). This sum captures the full transition from large base to small base.

4

Multiply by h/3. Dividing by 3 accounts for the tapering geometry — just like a pyramid holds one-third of a prism's volume.

🌍 Real-Life Uses

🏛️

Ancient Monuments

The base of the Pyramid of Giza and similar truncated structures are frustums of pyramids. Archaeologists and engineers use this formula to estimate their stone volume and total weight.

🏢

Building Foundations

Many tapered building foundations and column bases are frustum-shaped. Structural engineers calculate their concrete volume using this formula before pouring.

📦

Packaging Design

Tapered boxes and cartons — wider at the base for stability — are frustum-shaped. Packaging engineers calculate material volume and capacity using this formula.
