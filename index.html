The Three Figures

Description · Drawing · Formula

Frustum of a Cone

A cone with its top sliced off parallel to the base. It has two circular faces — a large base (R) and a smaller top (r) — connected by a slanted side.

Formula V = (1/3) × π × h × (R² + R·r + r²)
Frustum of a Pyramid

A pyramid with its top cut off. The bottom is a larger polygon area (A1) and the top is a smaller polygon area (A2), connected by trapezoidal sides.

Formula V = (h/3) × (A1 + A2 + √(A1 × A2))
Prismatoid

A general solid with two parallel bases (A1 and A2) and a middle cross-section (Am) measured halfway between them. It can approximate many irregular real-world shapes.

Formula (Simpson's Rule) V = (h/6) × (A1 + A2 + 4 × Am)
